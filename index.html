Menu

Albariño
Spanish white wine grape that makes crisp, refreshing, and light-bodied wines.
Barbaresco
A red wine from the Piedmont Region of Italy, made from Nebbiolo grapes it is lighter than Barolo.
Gamay
Beaujolais makes its famous, fruity reds exclusively from one of the many Gamays available, the Gamay Noir à Jus Blanc. Low in alcohol and relatively high in acidity, the wines are meant to be drunk soon after bottling.
Merlot
Herbs, Green Olive, Cherry & Chocolate. Softer & medium in weight with fewer tannins than Cabernet and ready to drink sooner. Takes well to Oak aging.
Nebbiolo
The great grape of Northern Italy, which excels there in Barolo and Barbaresco, strong, ageable wines. Mainly unsuccessful elsewhere, Nebbiolo also now has a small foothold in California.
Rosé
Sometimes called blush. Any light pink wine, dry to sweet, made by removing the skins of red grapes early in the fermentation process or by mixing red and white
Verdicchio
Italian white that produces a pale, light-bodied, crisp wine.
